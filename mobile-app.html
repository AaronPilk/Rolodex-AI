iOS reality

Step 1 of 9 · Sources
Connect Messages
Rolodex needs to read your iMessage history to learn how you talk to each person and when you last reached out.
  • Reads on-device only — never uploads message bodies
  • Indexes the people, dates, and your tone — not the content
  • You can revoke any time in Settings
How this works: Apple blocks iOS apps from reading Messages directly. We pair this with a free Mac companion app that runs in the background — your Mac does the indexing, your iPhone gets the digest via end-to-end encrypted iCloud sync.
Step 2 of 9 · Sources
Connect Gmail
Pull your email signals to spot life events, job changes, and warm contacts you've drifted from.
  • Multiple accounts — connect personal, work, side hustle
  • Tags accounts: personal vs business so tones don't bleed
  • Read-only access. Never sends without your approval.
How this works: Standard OAuth 2.0. Tap Allow → bounce to Google → grant read-only Gmail scope → bounce back. Works directly from this iPhone, no Mac required.
Step 3 of 9 · Sources
Connect Telegram
Pull your Telegram chats and groups to track relationships across the channel.
  • Reads DMs and group memberships
  • Auto-classifies group threads as their own circle
  • Direct connect — no Mac required
How this works: Telegram exposes a real user-account API (MTProto). You'll log into your Telegram on the next screen — just your phone number and the SMS code. From then on Rolodex syncs in the background.
Step 4 of 9 · Sources
Connect WhatsApp
Bring your WhatsApp DMs into your brain — including groups and international contacts.
  • One-time chat export — fully legal, doesn't break ToS
  • We'll show you exactly how to export — takes 30 seconds
  • Re-export anytime to refresh data
How this works: WhatsApp doesn't expose a personal-account API. The clean fix: in WhatsApp tap Settings → Chats → Export Chat → share to Rolodex. Manual but stable, doesn't violate ToS, and your data is safe.
Step 5 of 9 · Sources
Connect Instagram
Pull DMs, comments, and profile activity to track relationships in the channel where they live.
  • Captures DMs, story replies, and comments where you've engaged
  • Detects life events from posts (engagements, jobs, moves)
  • Read-only. Never likes, follows, or DMs without your approval.
How this works: Meta blocks DM API access for personal accounts. We use a Rolodex Chrome / Safari extension that captures messages and profile activity passively as you browse Instagram normally on your computer. No scraping, no automation — just observation.
Step 6 of 9 · Sources
Connect Messenger
Pull your Facebook Messenger DMs to find old friends drifting away.
  • The most powerful source for forgotten friendships
  • Birthday reminders auto-feed into priority list
  • Birthday-aware drafts that actually feel personal
How this works: Two paths: (1) the Chrome extension you just installed for Instagram works for Messenger too. (2) Or upload your Facebook data export (Settings → Your Information → Download Information → Messages). Same effect.
Step 7 of 9 · Sources
Connect TikTok & X
Track relationships across short-form social — including DM trails and profile signals.
  • X DMs via OAuth — same flow as Gmail
  • TikTok DMs via data export — request takes 24h
  • Add as you go — sources can be connected later
How this works: X provides v2 API access for personal DMs (paid tier). TikTok requires manual export from their privacy settings (delivered in 24 hours). Both supplement the bigger sources — fine to skip if you're not heavy on these platforms.
Step 8 of 9 · Sources
Contacts & Calendar
The classics — your iPhone Contacts and Calendar are the highest-signal life-event sources.
  • Contacts: enrich every relationship with name, photo, birthday
  • Calendar: spot when you're meeting them, when their birthdays are
  • Native iOS permission — instant, no setup
How this works: These are the only sources iOS lets us touch directly with full permission. Tap Allow → iOS shows the standard permission dialog → done.
